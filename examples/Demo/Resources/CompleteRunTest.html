Activities Preparation Guide:

Painting Workshop

1. Activity Summary & Goal

In this guided painting workshop, residents will be provided with watercolors and painting supplies to create their own artwork. The goal of the activity is to promote creativity, self-expression, and engagement in a relaxing and enjoyable setting. This activity is beneficial for residents as it helps improve cognitive function, fine motor skills, and overall well-being.

2. Preparation

  • Choose a spacious and well-lit room for the activity.
  • Arrange tables and chairs for the participants, ensuring there is enough space for each resident to work comfortably.
  • Cover the tables with plastic tablecloths to protect them from paint.
  • Prepare watercolor palettes, brushes, water containers, and paper towels for each participant.
  • Have a few examples of simple paintings for inspiration and guidance.

3. Shopping List

  • Watercolor paint sets: Purchase enough sets for each participant, ensuring a variety of colors and shades.
  • Brushes: Purchase various sizes of brushes for each participant.
  • Plastic tablecloths: Purchase enough to cover all tables being used for the activity.
  • Watercolor paper: Purchase enough sheets for each resident to create at least two paintings.
  • Paper towels: Purchase enough rolls for cleaning brushes and spills during the activity.
  • Water containers: Purchase one small container per participant for rinsing brushes.

4. Activity Guide

  • Welcome the residents to the painting workshop and provide them with their painting supplies.
  • Briefly explain the benefits of engaging in creative activities like painting.
  • Show the residents the example paintings for inspiration and explain that they can create their own unique artwork.
  • Demonstrate how to use the watercolor paints and brushes, and encourage the residents to experiment with different techniques.
  • Walk around the room, providing guidance and assistance as needed.
  • Encourage the residents to share their artwork with the group once they are finished.
  • At the end of the workshop, assist the residents in cleaning up and properly disposing of used materials.

Reminiscence Hour

1. Activity Summary & Goal

In this group session, residents will share stories and memories from their past, facilitated by a staff member. The goal of the activity is to encourage socialization and stimulate cognitive function while fostering a sense of connection among the residents. This activity is beneficial for those with memory loss as it helps them recall and share cherished memories.

2. Preparation

  • Choose a comfortable and quiet room for the reminiscence hour.
  • Arrange chairs in a circle to promote a sense of inclusivity and encourage conversation.
  • Prepare a list of open-ended questions or prompts to facilitate the discussion and help residents recall memories.

3. Shopping List

  • None. This activity requires no additional supplies as it is a conversation-based activity.

4. Activity Guide

  • Welcome the residents to the reminiscence hour and explain the purpose of the activity.
  • Invite the residents to sit in the circle of chairs and ensure everyone is comfortable.
  • Start the conversation by sharing a personal memory or anecdote.
  • Use the prepared list of questions or prompts to encourage residents to share their own memories.
  • Listen attentively to each story and ask follow-up questions to keep the conversation flowing.
  • Encourage other residents to ask questions or share related memories.
  • Be mindful of residents who may become emotional and provide support as needed.
  • At the end of the reminiscence hour, thank the residents for sharing their stories and encourage them to continue sharing memories with one another.
